Aura, a concept vehicle

For the European city commuter

The Context

I spent May-August 2010 working with nanotechnology research institute imec in Leuven, Belgium. (What a Euro trip that was!)

The Project

The aim was to design a concept vehicle with a futuristic aesthetic and high-technology integration. The target market was Europeans that commute in the big cities - crowded, dense, and restrictive to cars. The opportunity lay in the segment between bicycle commuters that are everywhere and the burdensome car commuters that are stuck in traffic.

Result: A fellow intern and I designed Aura, a 3-wheeled recumbed pedal-powered electric vehicle that aims to preseve the exhilaration of a bicycle ride and integrate the comfort of a car. Our user segment demanded this option if it was modern, attractive, and kept them less sweaty on the way to work. Aura is a sustainable creation, with a 'cradle-to-cradle' manufacturing cycle.

Features

Engineering design: rear-hub electric motor, the mechanical system, integration with pedal power. Controls system: allows the vehicle to decide when to provide electric-assist, smartphone integration (manufacturer-agnostic). Physical design: We sketched tens of initial designs of the interior and exterior.

Ecosystem: Aura is more than just a vehicle. It is an accessory, a partner in connecting with the community via the smartphone. The high-tech integration allows the modern-day commuter to embrace the power of personalization, activity tracking, and sustainable transportation.


The Reflection

I worked with an expert artist, engineering students at the GroepT university in Belgium, and my imagination. It was such a rewarding experience to design a system froma blank state.

The following summer, Aura was put into the prototype stage by imec. I hope vehicles like Aura come to life in the future.
