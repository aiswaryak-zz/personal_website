Tesla User Interface Design

Ideas on paper

The Context

I spent May-August 2013 at Tesla Motors in Mountain View, California. At the time of my internship, Tesla's EV sedan Model S was launching. As a testing intern, I got to watch fellow testers interact with the 17" touchscreen UI.

The Project

Watching others led me to make repeated observations about the way people use the touch screen. I started thinking about interaction - what do they enjoy, fumble with, and get confused about on the UI? What can be changed to better fit the user?

Result: I presented my top 4 UI paper prototypes to the UI manager at Tesla. He kept some of my prototypes and was interested in pursuing my suggestions in the future.

The Reflection

The opportunity to study the Model S UI taught me new lessons in human-machine interaction. Tesla take a different approach from other OEMs with the omission of physical buttons in the center stack. While the UI is beautiful and simple, I am concerned about the over-use of touch technology.

What is the best strategy for a car UI - will pure touchscreens distract the driver from the road? They will have to move their eyes off the road and to the screen. What compromises should be reached with tactile buttons and touch buttons? What about dynamically tactile buttons shown at CES 2014?

The overall experience of driving is still the central to the vehicle and we need to find satisfying, simple ways for the driver to enjoy their infotainment system.
