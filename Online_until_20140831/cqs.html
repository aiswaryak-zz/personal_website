CQS GroepT Racing Team

Building wireless data communication

The Context

I spent May-August 2010 working with a Belgian racing team at GroepT University in Leuven, Belgium.

The Project

The team developed vehicle with alternative energy drive trains - Pegasus on biethanol and Odyssee on electricity. Both cars participated in the 24h24' race for 2CVs at Spa-Francorchamps (home of the Belgian Grand Prix).

A fellow intern and I were helping them improve Odyssee before the big race. I worked on reliably transmitting vehicle data from the car on the track to the team in the pit. A second project involved making their software more modular.

Result: We successfully tested the wireless communication between the car and the pit at a test race at Circuite de Bresse in July. For the second project, I redesigned part of the software to make it more modular for future improvements.

Process

Wireless communication - I worked with Xbee modules to transmit data using the RS232 protocol between the car and pit. Wireless data was read, saved, and analyzed; all was done using LabView, mySQL, and Java. Incoming GPS data was plotted real-time on Google Maps to show the car's position on the track.

Software redesign - An interface was developed to modularly add new sensors and signals to the data monitoring system when Odyssee is updated in the future.


The Reflection

This was my first exposure to a startup-like environment. I learned lessons on feature ownership and agile development. I improved my skills in Java and picked up any new software skills as I needed them. Learning how to learn is a powerful life skill.
