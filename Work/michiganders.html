Michiganders and their cars

A photoessay of Michigan natives and their cars

The Context

I moved to Michigan about a year and 2 months ago - in July 2013. In the year I've been living here, not only do I see every third car on the highway is a Ford, GM, or Chrylser vehicle. I also notice how vehicle-centric Michigan events can be. During the summer and fall, there are tens of car shows (evidenced here). The largest event is the Dream Cruise on Woodward Avenue - a weekend full of antique cars, fascinating cars, floaters, rows of Mustangs from every year, and most of Southeast Michigan lounging on picnic chairs on the roadside to watch the drive by.

I've never an annual grand celebration of vehicles. But then again, where else in the world do you see three global automakers located miles from each other?

The Idea

Watching car enthusiasts at Ford, at the Woodward Dream cruise, and the Detroit Grand Prix is one thing. They are a certain breed that shares a passion in the complex machine that transports us everywhere. But, it led me to think about how my friends in Michigan - the HR representative, the financial analyst, the waiter, and nurse - see and imagine their vehicles. What do they feel about the car they drive? Do they love it? Hate it? Do they care at all?

I plan to curate a photo essay of these friends posing with their car. I wonder if the interaction with an inatimate object correlates to their interest/care for it. Alongside each photo, I will pose some standard questions on how they view the single most-used possession in their daily life.

(09/04/14): Photos will start showing up shortly, along with summarized responses from my friends.
